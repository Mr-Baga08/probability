# Probability Systems & Monte Carlo Simulations

> "In theory, there is no difference between theory and practice. In practice, there is."

This repository is a collection of mathematical models and computational simulations focused on probability, combinatorics, and system behaviour at scale. 

Rather than just calculating standard formulas, the goal of this project is to stress-test mathematical models against computational reality. We explore where analytical approximations (like Taylor series) hold true, where they accumulate compounding mathematical debt, and where they ultimately fracture against physical hardware limits (Machine Epsilon).

##  Project Philosophy

Mathematical formulas are models of reality. To trust a system architecture, we must prove it from first principles using two distinct approaches:
1. **Analytical Ground Truth:** Deriving the exact discrete probability limits.
2. **Monte Carlo Simulation:** Building digital twins of the experiments to verify the math through brute-force physical mechanics and the Law of Large Numbers.
